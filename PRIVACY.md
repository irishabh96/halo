# Privacy Policy - Halo

_Last updated: 22 June 2026_

Halo ("the extension") lets you ask ChatGPT from a Chrome side panel and refine the answer.
This policy explains exactly what data the extension handles and how.

## Summary

- Halo has **no backend server**. Everything runs locally in your browser.
- The only place your text is sent is **ChatGPT (OpenAI)** - through your own existing, logged-in
  `chatgpt.com` session, which is the core function of the extension.
- Halo does **not** send any data to the developer, and uses **no** analytics, tracking,
  advertising, or data selling.

## What data Halo handles

- **Your prompts and selected text.** When you type a question (or select text on a page and click
  "Ask Halo") and run it, that text is sent to ChatGPT via your logged-in `chatgpt.com` tab to
  generate a response.
- **The answer text** returned by ChatGPT, which is shown in the side panel.
- **Your ChatGPT session token.** To read answers from ChatGPT's own API, Halo uses the access token
  already present in your logged-in `chatgpt.com` session. This token is used **only** to call
  ChatGPT's API on `chatgpt.com`. It is never stored long-term and never sent to the developer or any
  other third party.

## Where data is stored

- Halo uses Chrome's **session storage** to temporarily keep your last question/answer and any text
  you sent from a page, so the panel can restore them while your browser session is open. This is
  cleared automatically when your browser session ends.
- The extension writes **no** data to any remote server.

## Page access

- To offer the "Ask Halo" button, Halo runs a small script on web pages to detect when you **select
  text**. It reads the current selection **only when you click "Ask Halo,"** and it does **not** read,
  collect, store, or transmit any other page content.
- On `chatgpt.com`, Halo interacts with the page to send your prompt and read the response.

## Third-party processing

- When you ask a question, your text is processed by **OpenAI / ChatGPT** under OpenAI's own terms
  and privacy policy. Halo does not control how OpenAI processes that data.
  See: https://openai.com/policies/privacy-policy

## Data sharing and sale

- Halo does **not** sell or transfer your data to third parties.
- Halo does **not** use your data for anything other than its single purpose (sending your text to
  ChatGPT and returning the answer).
- Halo does **not** use your data for creditworthiness or lending purposes.

## Changes

This policy may be updated; the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly.
